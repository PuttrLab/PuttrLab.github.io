Putting Is Half the Game. So Why Are You Practicing Blind?


Putting accounts for roughly 35–40% of all strokes in a round of golf. PGA Tour pros make only about 40% of their putts from 10 feet. For a 15-handicap amateur, that number drops far below 30%. The difference between a scratch golfer and a 15-handicap from a short range alone can be 10+ percentage points.

The path to lower scores isn't a new driver — it's a more consistent putting stroke. But until now, getting real data on your stroke meant spending $150–$300+ on shaft-mounted sensors or dedicated putting systems. Most golfers just guess what's wrong and hope for the best.

PuttrLab changes that.
